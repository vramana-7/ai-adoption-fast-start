# Discovery Question Framework

Use these questions to identify adoption blockers and collect enough signal to generate a practical 4-week onboarding plan.

## 1) Business Outcome and Scope
- What outcomes matter most in the next 30-60 days?
- Which department/team is in scope for the first rollout?
- What does "successful adoption" look like for leadership?

## 2) Current Workflow Baseline
- Which workflows are currently manual and repetitive?
- Where do delays, rework, or quality issues appear most often?
- Which workflow is easiest to pilot with low risk?

## 3) Current AI Maturity
- How often are team members currently using AI tools?
- What tasks are they already attempting with AI?
- What is working, and where are they getting stuck?

## 4) Trust, Risk, and Governance
- What are the primary concerns (privacy, accuracy, policy, IP)?
- Which data types or use cases are prohibited?
- What approvals are needed before broader rollout?

## 5) Enablement and Support
- Who are likely internal champions?
- How much time can participants spend weekly on onboarding?
- What manager behaviors are needed to reinforce adoption?

## 6) Confidence and Behavior Change
- What signals show rising confidence in AI-assisted work?
- What resistance patterns have you observed so far?
- What would make skeptical users willing to try again?

## 7) Measurement
- Which 2-3 metrics can be tracked during a 4-week pilot?
- How will baseline vs. week-4 performance be compared?
- Who owns reporting and retrospective review?

## Output Expectations

At the end of discovery, produce:
- top 3 blockers
- 1-2 pilot workflows
- key constraints
- readiness level by cohort
- measurable week-4 success signals
